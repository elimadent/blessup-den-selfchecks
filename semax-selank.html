The basics

What Semax and Selank actually are

Both are short peptides, just seven amino acids each, developed in Russia, and both carry a small Pro-Gly-Pro tail that makes them last longer in the body than a raw peptide would. That is where the similarity ends, because they were built from different parents for different jobs.

Semax: focus, from a piece of ACTH

Semax is an analog of a fragment of ACTH (the adrenal-signaling hormone), specifically the ACTH(4-10) region. The clever part of its design is what it leaves out: it keeps the brain-and-focus effects while dropping the part that would stimulate cortisol and the adrenal glands. In Russia it is an approved medicine for cognitive disorders, stroke recovery, and neuroprotection. The everyday interest is in focus, mental clarity, and motivation.

Selank: calm, from an immune peptide

Selank is an analog of tuftsin, a natural immune-signaling peptide. It is reached for as an anti-anxiety compound that, unlike a benzodiazepine, is described as calming without sedation, brain fog, or dependence. Because of its tuftsin origin it also carries genuine immune-modulating activity, a rare two-for-one. In Russia it is an approved medicine for anxiety.

The honest catch you will hear

"Approved medicine, so it must be proven." Half true. They are genuinely approved and used in Russia, which is more than almost any research peptide can say. But that approval rests on a body of largely Russian-language clinical work that Western regulators have not independently reviewed, and the healthy-adult "nootropic" use most people are curious about is extrapolated from patient studies, not proven in healthy people. Real history, thin independent proof. Hold both.

Which does what

Semax vs Selank, side by side

The two are almost mirror images, which is exactly why people run them together. The simplest way to hold it: Semax is the accelerator, Selank is the suspension.

SEMAX · FOCUS

The accelerator

Clean, caffeine-like mental lift without the jitters or crash. Works on BDNF and the melanocortin system. Morning only, because it is stimulating and can disrupt sleep if taken late.

SELANK · CALM

The suspension

Subtle calming, described as "calm focus" rather than blunting, with no sedation or dependence. Works on GABA and serotonin plus the immune system. Evening for sleep, anytime for daytime calm.

They act on different neurotransmitter systems with no direct antagonism, which is the whole rationale for the classic Russian co-prescribing pattern: Semax to drive the day, Selank to take the edge off. One important handling note that follows from the biology: do not co-time them, or you are pushing the accelerator and the brake at the same moment.

Under the hood

How they are thought to work

SEMAX 01

Raises BDNF

It activates melanocortin receptors in the brain and drives BDNF, the key "grow and rewire" neurotrophin, along with NGF, supporting plasticity, learning, and memory.

SEMAX 02

No cortisol cost

Because it uses only the ACTH(4-10) fragment, it delivers the focus and neuroprotection without the adrenal, cortisol-raising effect of the full hormone.

SELANK 01

Tunes GABA and serotonin

It shifts GABA sensitivity without binding the benzodiazepine site (why it does not sedate or cause dependence) and modulates serotonin, easing anxiety, plus a tuftsin-driven immune effect.

According to PubMed, a resting-state fMRI study in 52 healthy adults found both peptides changed connectivity between the amygdala and temporal cortex, giving the first direct neuroimaging read of a calm (Selank) and focus (Semax) signal in people (Panikratova 2020, doi:10.1134/S001249662001007X). A notable honest wrinkle for Semax: BDNF follows an inverted-U, so more is not better, past a point extra signaling can blunt the very plasticity you want.

Where the evidence actually stands

Real history, thin independent proof

Semax

Focus

Reached for clarity, motivation, and neuroprotection. Intranasal is the clinically validated route; some use subcutaneous. Morning only.

Calm

The Semax + Selank stack

Both

The classic pairing: Semax in the morning for drive, Selank in the evening for calm. The most well-precedented pairing in the peptide space, backed by decades of Russian co-prescribing.

Pros
  • Covers focus and calm on different systems, no antagonism
  • A balanced day without the overstimulation of a stimulant alone
  • The most-precedented pairing here
Cons
  • Two compounds means two sourcing and quality risks
  • Inherits both sets of caveats, thin independent proof included
  • Do not co-time them, gas and brake at once

Timing: Semax AM, Selank PM. Keep them apart in the day. Cycling (often 8 weeks on, 4 off) is community convention, not trial-derived; tolerance has not actually been documented for Selank.

The number you will see repeated

About those bioavailability figures

You will run into confident claims that Semax is "about 60% bioavailable intranasally" and Selank "about 92%." Treat both as unreliable. Those figures trace back to misreported rodent tracer studies, not real human measurements. What is actually solid is simpler: intranasal is the clinically validated route, the peptides clear the blood in minutes, and their effects last hours because the downstream changes (BDNF, gene expression, receptor sensitivity) outlast the molecule. Do not anchor a dose to a bioavailability percentage that was never really measured in people.

The takeaway

Plasma life is minutes; the effect is hours to a day. Consistency and route matter more than chasing a specific absorption number, and the honest answer to "what percent gets in" is that nobody has cleanly established it in humans.

Before you credit the peptide

Give the credit honestly

Focus and mood are especially easy to move with things that have nothing to do with a peptide, so ask what actually did the work.

  • Sleep, caffeine, and stress load. A good night's sleep and dialing back an over-caffeinated, over-stressed baseline will sharpen focus and calm nerves more than any peptide, and people often fix these at the same time they start one.
  • Expectation and novelty. Nootropics are a category where belief moves how you feel. That is not a knock, it is a reason to track honestly over weeks, not days.
  • A real, treatable issue. Persistent anxiety, low mood, or attention problems can have a specific cause and specific, effective care. A research peptide is not a substitute for getting that evaluated.

The foundation under everything

Focus and calm are built here first

Sleep

Nothing sharpens focus or steadies mood like consistent sleep, and nothing sabotages both faster than a shortage of it. Chasing a nootropic on five hours a night is working against your own brain. This is the highest-leverage free lever there is, and Semax dosed late will actively cost you here.

The stimulant audit

Before adding a focus compound, look honestly at the caffeine and stimulants already on board. Stacking Semax on top of an over-caffeinated baseline is how the jitters and irritability show up. Sometimes the real fix is less, not more.

Stress management that is not a molecule

Breathwork, movement, daylight, and actually addressing the source of chronic stress do for anxiety what Selank is only nudging. Selank can sit on top of that foundation; it cannot replace it.

Cutting through the noise

The honest safety read

Reassuring

General tolerability

Both are generally well tolerated in the reported use, and Selank notably lacks the dependence, tolerance, and withdrawal that make benzodiazepines a problem. That is a real advantage.

Watch

Semax: overstimulation and timing

Too high or too late, and you get irritability, anxiety, headache, or wrecked sleep. Morning, low, and short. Anecdotal hair loss with prolonged use is worth knowing.

Watch

Selank + serotonin medications

Because Selank modulates serotonin, combining it with an SSRI, SNRI, or MAOI is not well characterized. That is a clear "clear it with your provider first" situation, not a DIY stack.

Unknown

Long-term safety

Beyond the anecdotal Semax hair-loss note, there is little long-term data outside Russian clinical use. Pregnancy and breastfeeding have no safety data, so the honest answer there is avoid.

Skip

Selank for acute panic, or either as a mental-health substitute

Selank is not a fast rescue for a panic attack, and neither peptide is a replacement for real evaluation and care of an anxiety or mood disorder.

The honest bottom line

Where Semax and Selank honestly fit

If your foundation is handled, if you understand you are leaning on a real but largely un-replicated evidence base, and if you have a quality source, these are two of the more interesting research peptides to learn about, and among the better-tolerated. Selank in particular has the single most encouraging human result in this corner of the library, a benzo-comparable anxiety effect without the baggage. That is a real place.

It is also a humble one. The strongest evidence is for patients, not healthy optimizers; the bioavailability numbers are folklore; the cycling is convention, not science; and almost none of it has been confirmed outside one country. Held in that honest frame, Semax for a clean morning focus and Selank for a steady calm are worth understanding well, as a modest, provider-guided experiment on top of sleep and stress you have already handled.

See a professional promptly, not a peptide

Focus and mood problems can be signs of something that needs real care. If any of these fit, get evaluated first.

  • Panic attacks or overwhelming, disabling anxiety
  • Any thoughts of self-harm or hopelessness
  • A sudden change in personality, memory, or thinking
  • Anxiety or low mood that is steadily worsening
  • New attention problems that are disrupting your life
  • Anything that feels clearly beyond "everyday stress"

These deserve a real evaluation, not a nootropic. If you are in crisis, reach out to a crisis line or emergency services now. Getting the right help is the fastest path forward, every time.

The BlessUp take

Real history, honest limits

Semax and Selank are more interesting than most of the peptide world because they are not just theory, they are prescribed, used, and studied in real clinics. That earns genuine respect. But a track record in one country is not the same as proof the rest of the world can lean on, the healthy-adult focus story is extrapolated, and the numbers people quote are shakier than they sound. The strong move is to hold both truths: take the real history seriously, and keep the honest limits just as close. Handle sleep, stress, and stimulants first, involve a provider (especially with any serotonin medication), verify your source, and treat it as a modest experiment, not a proven upgrade.
